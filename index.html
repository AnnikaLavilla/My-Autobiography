Hello, everyone! My name is Annika Julia Lavilla. I was born on July 9, 2005, making me 18 years old as of now. It's incredible how time flies, isn't it?

I come from a family where education is highly valued, and I am fortunate to have parents who have always encouraged me to pursue my dreams. Growing up, I was always curious about the world around me, eager to learn and explore new things.

I spent my formative years studying at MIT International School, where I've had the opportunity to engage with a diverse community of students and teachers. The school has not only provided me with a solid academic foundation but also instilled in me a love for learning and a sense of curiosity that continues to drive me forward.

Outside of academics, I have a variety of interests and hobbies. I enjoy reading books on a wide range of topics, from science and technology to literature and philosophy. I also have a passion for music and have been learning to play the ukulele since I was young. I love staying fit, and have been playing football since I was 12 years old. But what I love most is painting and expressing myself through my art. In my free time, you can often find me exploring nature, taking runs, or capturing moments through photography.

My Childhood

Growing up, I was called Kai or Ikai for my grandparents. I'm proud to say that I was an obedient child. I was always quiet and never really threw tantrums. I enjoyed spending time at the beach and eating with my grandparents. I have 3 siblings and I am the second eldest. Growing up with them was like living with instant playmates. Despite the fact that they always made me cry, I love my siblings very much and now that we are older we have a better relationship.

I started going to MIT International School at the age of 7 years old when I entered 1st Grade. Here I met my bestfriend, Angel. We were the polar opposites of each other. I am very grateful for her. Without her, I would have not made it through gradeschool and almost the entirety of my highschool. I created good relationships here in school, some of my teachers from gradeschool I still have contact with, and I get to share good memories I had with them.


I was never academically smart, but I knew how to work hard for the things that I like to achieve.
